Insights from Customer Data

Contract Type Impact

Month-to-month customers are significantly more likely to churn compared to those on longer-term contracts. Two-year contracts show the highest retention rates.

Internet Service Patterns

Fiber optic customers have higher churn rates than DSL users, possibly due to higher costs or service quality expectations.

Tenure Matters

Customers with shorter tenure (less than 12 months) are more likely to churn, while long-term customers (24+ months) show stronger loyalty.

Payment Method Influence

Customers using electronic checks have higher churn rates, while those with automatic payment methods (credit card/bank transfer) show better retention.

Monthly Charges Impact

Higher monthly charges correlate with increased churn risk, especially for customers paying over $80 per month.

Service Bundle Effect

Customers with multiple services (phone, internet, streaming) and additional features (security, tech support) show lower churn rates.
